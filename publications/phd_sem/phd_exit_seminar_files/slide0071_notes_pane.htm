Currently second generation mobile phone systems are in operation. These phones primarily provide voice services, with very limited data applications. Third generations systems will be introduced world wide around 2003. These systems will provide a data rate ranging from 144 kbps to 2 Mbps depending on the distance from the base station and the velocity of the user. The principle aim of third generation systems is to provide data services, allowing high speed mobile internet terminals, along with video phone communication. Third generation systems use an RF carrier modulation scheme called Direct Sequence Code Division Multiple Access, which is similar to the current CDMA systems in operation, such as IS-95. Third generation systems use a wide bandwidth of 5 MHz to achieve a high data rate.
