OFDM has many advantages over single carrier modulation schemes. These include a strong multipath tolerance, flexible channel optimisation, and a high spectral efficiency. The flexibility of OFDM is a result of the subcarrier independence in modulation scheme allocation.

Due to the multi-carrier nature of OFDM it is more sensitive to frequency tracking errors, Doppler shift (frequency offset due to movement of the transmitter or receiver), Doppler spread (frequency modulation due to movement of the transmitter or receiver in a multipath channel), and phase noise. OFDM requires the use of linear amplification to prevent spectral spreading, which is caused by intermodulation distortion creating unwanted out of band spectral components. The required linearity of the amplification depends on the subcarrier modulation scheme. There is a direct trade off between the linearity required and the spectral efficiency.
Another disadvantage of OFDM is that the envelope of the time waveform can vary over a large range. This results in a large peak to average power ratio.
