The results of the measurements show the wide bandwidth fading pattern of the channel. From this we can see nulls in the spectrum (shown in dark red) and the peaks in the response (in yellow white). The dotted line shows the mean path loss.
This result shows the response for a Line Of Sight (LOS) transmission. As a result of this the direct signal tends to dominate and consequently the delay spread of the channel is low. When the delay spread of the channel is low the frequency separation between the nulls tends to be large (coherence bandwidth), as we can see here. At 95 cm along there are no nulls even over a bandwidth of 70 MHz.
