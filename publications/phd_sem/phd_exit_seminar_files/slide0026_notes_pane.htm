Most Wireless LAN systems use a single transmission location to provide coverage over the area of a building or coverage of a small group of buildings. Providing this coverage from a single transmission location is difficult due to shadowing caused by walls, and objects in the building.
Access Point Repeaters is a simple method for providing reduced pathloss in areas that suffer from significant shadowing. The signal from the base station is transmitted (and received) from multiple locations. Each transmitter appears as multipath to the mobile receiver, however provided the delay spread is shorter than the guard period of the OFDM signal then the multipath signals increase the received signal power, while not resulting in inter-symbol interference.
