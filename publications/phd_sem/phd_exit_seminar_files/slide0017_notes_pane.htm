The same occurs in the frequency domain. As the frequency is changed the wavelength of the RF signal also changes. This results in the phase of the received multipath components changing, and correspondingly the interference between them.
Frequency selective fading results in errors in OFDM signals as subcarriers that occur in nulls have a very low SNR. By tracking the nulls in the spectrum it is possible to avoid transmitting in them, saving transmission power and minimising the error rate.
