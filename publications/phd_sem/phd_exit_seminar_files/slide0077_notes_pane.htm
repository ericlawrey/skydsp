A raytraced simulation was used to estimate the further improvements that could be made using more transmitters. Increasing the number of APRs results in a reduced pathloss because it reduces the average distance to a transmitter. When a single transmitter is used in free space the signal power reduces in proportion with the distance squared. If we took the logical extension of increasing the number of APR, then we would end up with a plane of transmission, with the power spread evenly over the inner surface of the building’s ceiling. When close to this plane (as is the case when inside the building) the power decreases in proportion to the distance, as a result the pathloss will be half (in dB) to free space loss.
